
Otherworldly Patron: The Ten Shadows

Your soul is bound to a clan technique older than you — ten beasts of living shadow that answer your call and, when you push too hard, answer no one.

Shadow Points (your core resource)

You have a pool of Shadow Points (SP) equal to your warlock level. You regain all SP on a long rest. Summoning a shadow costs SP equal to its cost and keeps it on the field until it drops to 0 HP, you dismiss it (no action), or you fall unconscious. You can have any number out as long as you can pay for them.

Summoned shadows act on your turn (you decide their move + action), use your spell save DC, and have HP scaling with your level so they don’t evaporate but also can’t tank forever.

Stress & Blackout (the backfire)

Every shadow has a will. Pushing the technique builds Stress, tracked 0–20.

You gain 1 Stress when you: summon a shadow while already at 0 SP, re-summon a shadow that died this combat, or force-tame a new shadow (see Expansion below).

	•	At Stress 10+, at the start of each of your turns make a DC 16 Constitution save. On a failure you Black Out.
	•	Blackout (genuinely dangerous): the DM controls your character for 1d4 rounds. You attack the nearest creature — ally or enemy — with Bane (subtract 1d4 from attack rolls and saves). Any summon during blackout is chosen by the DM, not you. At the start of each blacked-out turn roll a d20; on a 20 you snap out early. Stress resets to 0 when blackout ends.
	•	Stress decreases by 1 per short rest and resets to 0 on a long rest.

This is the cost-control valve: you can overcast, but doing it repeatedly risks turning your own beasts (and Bane’d attacks) on your party.

The Shadows

Costs are in SP. Higher-cost shadows are gated by warlock level so a level 3 character can’t field an elephant.

Level 3 (1 SP each)

	•	Divine & Demon Dogs — summon as a pair. Demon Dog has advantage on its attacks; Divine Dog can take the Help action as a bonus action. If one dies, the survivor gains the other’s trait.
	•	Toad (Gama) — reaction grapple: pulls a creature within 15 ft toward you (Str save vs your DC) and can hold it.
	•	Rabbit Escape — functions as Silent Image; the swarm also imposes disadvantage on one attack against you as a reaction.
	•	Nue (min level 5) — flying scout. Reaction lightning shield grants you resistance to one attack; once per long rest its lightning works as Chain Lightning.

Level 6 (2 SP each)

	•	Great Serpent — bite grapple that restrains as Hold Monster (target still saves each turn).
	•	Max Elephant (3 SP) — water blast equals Tidal Wave once per long rest; trunk slam knocks back 10 ft.

Level 9 (3 SP each)

	•	Piercing Ox — charge attack adds +1d6 force per 30 ft moved before the hit, capped at +4d6.
	•	Round Deer — on summon, heals as Mass Healing Word; while present, one ally regains 1d6 HP at the start of your turn.

Level 13 (5 SP)

	•	Toad-Tiger / Max Mode — upgrades both Dogs to dire (double HP, +1d6 damage), lets you summon one 1-SP shadow as a bonus action while it’s out, and once per long rest it makes a meteor-pounce (2d10 force in a 15-ft radius, Dex save).

Shadow Taming Ritual

You begin with both the Divine Dog and Demon Dog already bound to you (or a shadow of the DM and player's choosing). Every other shadow must be tamed through ritual before it can be summoned.

Performing the Ritual
Before taking a long rest, you may initiate the Shadow Taming Ritual. You and any willing party members sit in a circle. After 10 minutes of channeling, all participants are transported to a pocket shadow realm — a lightless void where the target shadow waits. The ritual ends (successfully or not) before the long rest begins.

The Fight
The shadow fights at its full stat block. Your already-tamed shadows may assist you. When the target shadow is reduced to half its maximum HP it submits and is permanently added to your shadow list.

Party Scaling
Each party member beyond yourself who enters the realm makes the shadow stronger. For each additional participant: +1 to the shadow's attack rolls and saving throw DCs, and +15% to its maximum HP (rounded up). Tamed shadows that assist do not increase the difficulty.

Failure
If the party is defeated or retreats, you lose one spell slot of the highest level available and suffer disadvantage on all saving throws until your next long rest. The shadow cannot be challenged again until after that long rest.

Subclass features by level

	•	Lvl 3 — Shadow as Shield: reaction, a dog interposes for +2 AC against one attack.
	•	Lvl 6 — Shadow Walk: Misty Step through any shadow you control, no spell slot, twice per long rest.
	•	Lvl 10 — Hidden in Shadow: you can hide inside a summoned shadow — total cover, and you can attack out of it without breaking it.
	•	Lvl 14 — Chimera Shadow Garden (Domain): as an action, for 1 minute summons cost 0 SP and you may field any shadow on your list. Activating it immediately sets your Stress to 5. Once per long rest.

MAHORAGA & the Dad clause (DM-gated, never castable)

Mahoraga is not on your shadow list. You cannot choose to summon it. It only appears on a catastrophic blackout: when you reach Stress 20.

When you hit Stress 20, your body is taken over — the old man who once tried and failed to tame it, who happens to be your character’s father. The DM runs Mahoraga as an NPC against the entire table, including you when you regain your body. Its adaptation works as your d100 idea: each round, roll d100; on a 50+ it gains immunity or resistance to the last damage type / effect that hit it (DM’s call).
